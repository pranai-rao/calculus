Integration by Parts, Part 2

Example

  1. \(\int x^2e^{-x}dx\)

    2. \(\begin{align}u &= x^2 \\ du &= 2xdx\end{align}\) \(\begin{align}dv &= e^{-x}dx \\ v &= -e^x\end{align}\)

    $$\begin{align}\int udv &= uv- \int vdu \\ \int x^2e^{-x}dx &= -x^2e^{-x}+\int2xe^{-x}dx\end{align}$$

    \(\begin{align}u &= 2x \\ du &= 2dx\end{align}\) \(\begin{align}dv &= e^{-x}dx \\ v &= -e^x\end{align}\)

    $$\begin{align}\int x^2e^{-x}dx &= -x^2e^{-x}+\bigg{[}-2xe^{-x}-\int-2e^{-x}dx\bigg{]} \\ &= -x^2e^{-x}-2xe^{-x}+\int2e^{-x}dx \\ &= -x^2e^{-x}-2xe^{-x}-2e^{-x}+C\end{align}$$

Sometimes it is necessary to use IbP more than once. When we have integrals in the form \(\int p(x)f(x)dx\) where \(p(x)\) is a polynomial, can sometimes be evaluated using repeated IbP where \(u=p(x)\), or one of its derivatives, at each stage.

Since \(p(x)\) is a polynomial, repeated differentiation will eventually produce zeroâ€”at which point you will be left with a simplified integration problem.

Organizing this can be done with tabular IbP.

Tabular IbP

Steps

  1. Differentiate \(p(x)\) until you obtain zero. List in the first column.
  2. Integrate \(f(x)\) repeatedly in the second column to match all rows in the first column.
  3. Draw an arrow from each entry in the first column to one row down in the second column.
  4. Label arrows with an alternating \(+\) and \(-\) sign. Always start with the \(+\) sign.
  5. For each arrow, form the product at its tip and end and its corresponding \(+/-\) sign. Sum the products for the result.

Example

  1. \(\int(x^2-x)\cos(x)dx\)

    2. u

    dv
    \(x^2-x\) \(\searrow^+\) \(\cos(x)\)
    \(2x-1\) \(\searrow^-\) \(\sin(x)\)
    \(2\) \(\searrow^+\) \(-\cos(x)\)
    \(0\) \(\rightarrow\) \(-\sin(x)\)

$$\int (x^2-x)\cos(x)dx=(x^2-x)\sin(x)+(2x-1)\cos(x)-2\sin(x)+C$$

What if neither function is a polynomial?

$$\int e^x\cos(x)dx$$

No matter what you choose for \(u\), it will never come to zero. IbP still works, however, and tabular IbP can help us.

Example

  1. \(\int e^x\cos(x)dx\)

    2. u

    dv
    \(e^x\) \(\searrow^+\) \(\cos(x)\)
    \(e^x\) \(\searrow^-\) \(\sin(x)\)
    \(e^x\) \(\rightarrow\) \(-\cos(x)\)

$$\begin{align}\int e^x\cos(x)dx &= e^x\sin(x)+e^x\cos(x)-\int e^x\cos(x)dx \\ 2\int e^x\cos(x)dx &= e^x\sin(x)+e^x\cos(x) \\ \int e^x\cos(x)dx &= \frac{1}{2}e^x(\sin(x)+\cos(x))+C\end{align}$$
